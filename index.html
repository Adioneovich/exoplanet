  • Kepler-90

    The Kepler-90 star system consists of eight known exoplanets, each with unique characteristics. All planets orbit their star very closely, making their orbital periods (or "years") much shorter than those of planets in the Solar System. Let's look at each planet in this system

  • Kepler-90 b

    Kepler-90 b Radius: 1.32 Earth radii. Orbital period: 7.45 days. Temperature: Very high, around 750°C. Type: Rocky planet. Description: This is the closest planet to the star, slightly larger than Earth. Due to its proximity to the star, the temperature is extremely high, making it uninhabitable.

  • Kepler-90 c

    Kepler-90 c Radius: 2.88 Earth radii. Orbital period: 8.72 days. Temperature: Around 430°C. Type: Likely a mini-Neptune or a gas planet. Description: This planet is significantly larger than Earth and orbits slightly further from the star, but still very close by Solar System standards. Its surface temperature is also too high for life.

  • Kepler-90 i

    Kepler-90 i Radius: 1.32 Earth radii. Orbital period: 14.45 days. Temperature: Around 430°C. Type: Rocky planet. Description: One of the smallest planets in the system, similar in size to Earth, but its temperature makes it hostile to life.

  • Kepler-90 d

    Kepler-90 d Radius: 3.88 Earth radii. Orbital period: 59.74 days. Temperature: Around 290°C. Type: Likely a mini-Neptune. Description: This planet is much larger than Earth and is probably a gas or ice giant. Its orbital period is longer, but still not enough to reduce its temperature to habitable levels.

  • Kepler-90 e

    Kepler-90 e Radius: 1.52 Earth radii. Orbital period: 91.94 days. Temperature: Around 220°C. Type: Rocky planet. Description: This planet is classified as a "super-Earth," slightly larger than Earth, and it orbits the star in about 92 days. While its temperature is lower than that of the inner planets, it is still too high for life.

  • Kepler-90 f

    Kepler-90 f Radius: 2.83 Earth radii. Orbital period: 124.91 days. Temperature: Around 160°C. Type: Likely a mini-Neptune. Description: This planet is also likely a gas giant. Its orbit is longer, but the temperature is still too high for the existence of liquid water and life.

  • Kepler-90 g

    Kepler-90 g Radius: 8.12 Earth radii (similar to Jupiter in size). Orbital period: 210.60 days. Temperature: Around 120°C. Type: Gas giant. Description: This planet resembles Jupiter and is one of the gas giants in the system. Though its orbital period is longer, the temperature is still too high for life.

  • Kepler-90 h

    Kepler-90h Radius: 11.32 Earth radii. Orbital period: 331.60 days. Temperature: Around -40°C. Type: Gas giant. Description: This is the farthest planet in the system, similar to Jupiter or Saturn. Its orbital period is nearly the length of a year on Earth, and its temperature is much cooler than the other planets. However, it is still a gas giant, making habitable conditions unlikely.

Planets of star system : Kepler-90

Kepler-90 b

Kepler-90b is a super Earth exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 2.27 Earths, it takes 7 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.074 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.

Kepler-90 c

Kepler-90c is a super Earth exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 1.81 Earths, it takes 8.7 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.089 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.

Kepler-90 i

Kepler-90 i is a super Earth exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 2.3 Earths, it takes 14.4 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.120138 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2017.

Kepler-90 d

Kepler-90 d is a Neptune-like exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 8.6 Earths, it takes 59.7 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.32 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.

Kepler-90 e

Kepler-90 e is a Neptune-like exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 7.56 Earths, it takes 91.9 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.42 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.

Kepler-90 f

Kepler-90 f is a Neptune-like exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 8.65 Earths, it takes 124.9 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.48 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.

Kepler-90 g

Kepler-90 g is a gas giant exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 0.8 Jupiters, it takes 210.6 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.71 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.

Kepler-90 h

Kepler-90 h is a gas giant exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 1.2 Jupiters, it takes 331.6 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 1.01 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2013.
